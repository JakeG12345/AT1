The Internet - Types of Protocols

Transfer Protocols

The internet is made up of over 100 protocols, with HTTP, SMTP, and FTP being the most common. HTTP is used for the World Wide Web, SMTP for email delivery, and FTP for file transfers. FTP allows for the upload and download of files, including programs, graphics, sounds, video, and text files. Downloads are often restricted and large files are compressed to save space on FTP servers. FTP programs are often required to upload files for personal websites.

Communication Protocols

The TCP/IP protocol used by every computer on the Internet actually consists of two separate protocols. The first is the Internet Protocol, which assigns a unique IP address to each computer on the Internet. This address consists of four numbers separated by periods.

Packets

Data transmission on the internet involves breaking down messages into packets of fewer than 1500 characters each, which are sent via the best available route to avoid congestion. These packets are then reassembled at their destination using the information in the packet. This method of data transmission is called packet-switched networks, which include the sender's and receiver's IP addresses, the total number of packets, and the number of each packet. TCP/IP is used to transmit data and continues to be active until the user logs off the internet.
